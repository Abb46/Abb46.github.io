Base Jumping


Base Jumping also sometimes written as B.A.S.E jumping is parachuting or wingsuit flying from a fixed structure or cliff. "BASE" is an acronym that stands for four categories of fixed objects from which one can jump: building,antenna, span and earth. Due to the lower altitudes of the jumps, BASE jumping is significantly more dangerous than skydiving from a plane. In the US, BASE jumping is regarded by many as a fringe or extreme sport. In some locations and jurisdictions BASE jumping is prohibited or illegal. However in some places it is permitted.
